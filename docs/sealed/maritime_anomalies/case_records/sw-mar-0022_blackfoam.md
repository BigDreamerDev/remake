## Summary

At Observation Deck 4, staff recorded a waterline displacement event lasting between 64
minutes and 3 hours. The occurrence was initially treated as a maintenance failure, then
reclassified when the water failed to obey floor gradient, drain position, or witness
expectation.
## Initial Report

The first report describes a clean smell of salt in a closed interior space. Personnel
present stated that the sound of distant surf became audible before any visible moisture
appeared. The nearest exterior waterline was logged as 362 metres away, though the incident
room later showed barnacle marks at ankle height.
One witness noted that the reflection in the water showed a stairwell descending through the
ceiling. No witness was able to agree on whether the reflected space was frightening or
familiar. This disagreement is considered part of the event, not a failure of interviewing.

## Irregular Details

The water temperature was recorded as 6°C, except by one thermometer that reported 'low
tide' instead of a number. A paper cup left on the floor moved 18 cm against the visible
current. Audio equipment captured no speech, but the waveform resembles a person remembering
a song without singing it.
Recovered residue contained salt, dust, printer toner, and a grey fibre matching no approved
uniform. The fibre was dry at the centre and wet along both ends. The lab refused the second
sample after the envelope began dripping upward inside the evidence drawer.
## Follow-Up

Recommended administrative action: append to the washroom recurrence ledger. Final notation:
vessel name removed from harbour registry. No operational key, passphrase, route, or command
authority is contained in this record.
