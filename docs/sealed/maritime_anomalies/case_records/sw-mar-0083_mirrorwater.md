## Summary

At Blackwater Pier, staff recorded a language in condensation event lasting between 82
minutes and 5 hours. The occurrence was initially treated as a maintenance failure, then
reclassified when the water failed to obey floor gradient, drain position, or witness
expectation.
## Initial Report

The first report describes a clean smell of salt in a closed interior space. Personnel
present stated that the sound of distant surf became audible before any visible moisture
appeared. The nearest exterior waterline was logged as 531 metres away, though the incident
room later showed barnacle marks at ankle height.
One witness noted that the reflection in the water showed open sea under fluorescent light.
No witness was able to agree on whether the reflected space was frightening or familiar.
This disagreement is considered part of the event, not a failure of interviewing.
## Containment Guidance

9. Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
7. If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
9. No bell is to be answered from below deck.
7. If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.

## Irregular Details

The water temperature was recorded as 4°C, except by one thermometer that reported 'low
tide' instead of a number. A paper cup left on the floor moved 21 cm against the visible
current. Audio equipment captured no speech, but the waveform resembles a person remembering
a song without singing it.
Recovered residue contained salt, dust, printer toner, and a grey fibre matching no approved
uniform. The fibre was dry at the centre and wet along both ends. The lab refused the second
sample after the envelope began dripping upward inside the evidence drawer.
