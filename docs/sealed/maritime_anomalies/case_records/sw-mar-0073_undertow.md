## Summary

At Grey Buoy Field, staff recorded a mirror water event lasting between 4 minutes and 8
hours. The occurrence was initially treated as a maintenance failure, then reclassified when
the water failed to obey floor gradient, drain position, or witness expectation.
## Initial Report

The first report describes a clean smell of salt in a closed interior space. Personnel
present stated that the sound of distant surf became audible before any visible moisture
appeared. The nearest exterior waterline was logged as 512 metres away, though the incident
room later showed barnacle marks at ankle height.
One witness noted that the reflection in the water showed the same corridor at night. No
witness was able to agree on whether the reflected space was frightening or familiar. This
disagreement is considered part of the event, not a failure of interviewing.

tide' instead of a number. A paper cup left on the floor moved 21 cm against the visible
