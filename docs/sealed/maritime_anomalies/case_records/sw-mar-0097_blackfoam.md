## Summary

At Oyster Stair, staff recorded a tidal duplication event lasting between 5 minutes and 5
hours. The occurrence was initially treated as a maintenance failure, then reclassified when
the water failed to obey floor gradient, drain position, or witness expectation.
## Initial Report

The first report describes a clean smell of salt in a closed interior space. Personnel
present stated that the sound of distant surf became audible before any visible moisture
appeared. The nearest exterior waterline was logged as 644 metres away, though the incident
room later showed barnacle marks at ankle height.
One witness noted that the reflection in the water showed open sea under fluorescent light.
No witness was able to agree on whether the reflected space was frightening or familiar.
This disagreement is considered part of the event, not a failure of interviewing.
