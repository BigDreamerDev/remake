## Summary

At Lower Bell Marina, staff recorded a standing wave event lasting between 80 minutes and 9
hours. The occurrence was initially treated as a maintenance failure, then reclassified when
the water failed to obey floor gradient, drain position, or witness expectation.
## Initial Report

The first report describes a clean smell of salt in a closed interior space. Personnel
present stated that the sound of distant surf became audible before any visible moisture
appeared. The nearest exterior waterline was logged as 444 metres away, though the incident
room later showed barnacle marks at ankle height.
One witness noted that the reflection in the water showed a person standing where no person
stood. No witness was able to agree on whether the reflected space was frightening or
familiar. This disagreement is considered part of the event, not a failure of interviewing.
## Containment Guidance

1. Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
2. Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
2. Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
9. Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Irregular Details

The water temperature was recorded as 5°C, except by one thermometer that reported 'low
tide' instead of a number. A paper cup left on the floor moved 9 cm against the visible
current. Audio equipment captured no speech, but the waveform resembles a person remembering
a song without singing it.
Recovered residue contained salt, dust, printer toner, and a grey fibre matching no approved
uniform. The fibre was dry at the centre and wet along both ends. The lab refused the second
sample after the envelope began dripping upward inside the evidence drawer.
## Follow-Up

Recommended administrative action: seal the intake key in wax. Final notation: no further
entry permitted below the marked waterline. No operational key, passphrase, route, or
command authority is contained in this record.
