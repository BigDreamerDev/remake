## Summary

At Anchor Hall, staff recorded a reverse tide event lasting between 25 minutes and 7 hours.
The occurrence was initially treated as a maintenance failure, then reclassified when the
water failed to obey floor gradient, drain position, or witness expectation.
## Initial Report

The first report describes a clean smell of salt in a closed interior space. Personnel
present stated that the sound of distant surf became audible before any visible moisture
appeared. The nearest exterior waterline was logged as 725 metres away, though the incident
room later showed barnacle marks at ankle height.
One witness noted that the reflection in the water showed a person standing where no person
stood. No witness was able to agree on whether the reflected space was frightening or
familiar. This disagreement is considered part of the event, not a failure of interviewing.
## Containment Guidance

1. Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
9. Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
4. Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
5. If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.

## Irregular Details

The water temperature was recorded as 8°C, except by one thermometer that reported 'low
tide' instead of a number. A paper cup left on the floor moved 27 cm against the visible
current. Audio equipment captured no speech, but the waveform resembles a person remembering
a song without singing it.
Recovered residue contained salt, dust, printer toner, and a grey fibre matching no approved
uniform. The fibre was dry at the centre and wet along both ends. The lab refused the second
sample after the envelope began dripping upward inside the evidence drawer.
## Follow-Up

Recommended administrative action: mark as weather unless the rain falls upward. Final
notation: watch rotation extended indefinitely. No operational key, passphrase, route, or
command authority is contained in this record.
