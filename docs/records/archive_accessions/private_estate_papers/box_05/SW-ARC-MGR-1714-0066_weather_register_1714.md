# 1714 Weather Register — Navigation Deposition

**Accession:** SW-ARC-MGR-1714-0066
**Custody:** Office of Inherited Silences
**Current Location:** Pondroom
**Material:** map cloth
**Hand:** unknown documentary hand
**Status:** stable while unopened

## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a weather register and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1714, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: signature duplicated after drying.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

A witness named in the margin appears in two separate centuries of the archive without change of address.

A historical seal appears in the correct position, but the impression is reversed, as if pressed from beneath the page.

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

The lower margin contains a waterline that does not correspond to any known flood level.

## Handling

- The original is not to be requested unless the copy asks first.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.

## Extract

> A map of the coast includes an inland corridor labelled only as return.

## Archive Decision

Transfer a copy to Wet Copy Intake and leave the original where it believes it is.
