# 1324 Glass-Plate Caption — Charter Copy

**Accession:** SW-ARC-QRY-1324-0123
**Custody:** Office of Inherited Silences
**Current Location:** Sublevel 5
**Material:** vellum
**Hand:** machine copy
**Status:** returned twice

## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a glass-plate caption and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1324, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: reverse side warmer than front.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

The lower margin contains a waterline that does not correspond to any known flood level.

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

A docket note refers to the blank visitor entry. This may be administrative noise.

A historical seal appears in the correct position, but the impression is reversed, as if pressed from beneath the page.

## Handling

- The original is not to be requested unless the copy asks first.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.

## Extract

> The harbour was still when we arrived, and the harbourmaster refused to put his name below the line.

## Archive Decision

Index under historical holdings. Do not reconcile with living personnel records.
