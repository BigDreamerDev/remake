# 1996 Chapel Account — Digitisation Exception Packet

**Custody:** Sigillography Bay

**Hand:** late chancery

## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a chapel account and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1996, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: pagination continues beyond final folio.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

A historical seal appears in the correct position, but the impression is reversed, as if pressed from beneath the page.

The document references Mossgate as if it were a civic location rather than an internal Foundation designation.

The lower margin contains a waterline that does not correspond to any known flood level.

## Handling

- All salt damage is to be treated as prior witness.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.

## Extract

> The parish bell was heard beneath the paper. No bell is listed in the parish account.

## Archive Decision

Retain under routine historical accessions. No further action unless the item requests comparison.
