# 1488 Court Roll — Dockside Witness Ledger

**Accession:** SW-ARC-PAL-1488-0023
**Custody:** Palaeography Alcove
**Current Location:** Driftmoor
**Material:** browned receipt paper
**Hand:** unknown documentary hand
**Status:** indexed but not reconciled

## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a court roll and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1488, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: waterline visible at lower margin.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

A docket note refers to the lower switchboard. This may be administrative noise.

A historical seal appears in the correct position, but the impression is reversed, as if pressed from beneath the page.

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

A witness named in the margin appears in two separate centuries of the archive without change of address.

## Handling

- Records addressed to the seventh shelf are to be placed on the sixth.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.

## Extract

> The harbour was still when we arrived, and the harbourmaster refused to put his name below the line.

## Archive Decision

Retain under routine historical accessions. No further action unless the item requests comparison.
