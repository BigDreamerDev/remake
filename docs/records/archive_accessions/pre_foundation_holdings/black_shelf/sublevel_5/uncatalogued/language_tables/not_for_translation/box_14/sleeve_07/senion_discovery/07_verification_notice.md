# Verification Notice — Pre-Threshold Tongue

**Form:** SW-COD-VER-001
**From:** Codicology working group
**To:** Reading-room staff who have completed Documents α and β

You are now eligible to attempt verification.

## How to present

At a comShell terminal, invoke:

```
senion.verify
```

The gate will introduce itself, then put thirteen questions to you in sequence. Each question is one of the following kinds.

1. **Particle identification.** *"`-nai` indicates the speaker…"* The expected answer is a short phrase or one or two words.
2. **Postposition identification.** *"In `arvel-eth`, the suffix `-eth` indicates…"* The expected answer is a short English gloss.
3. **Tense or mood identification.** *"The suffix `-vor` adds what to the clause?"* Same format.
4. **Translation, Senion → English.** *"Translate: `Kor han-seln.`"* The expected answer is the English meaning, in any clear phrasing. The gate accepts many wordings of the same sense.
5. **Composition, English → Senion.** *"Render in Senion: 'The door exists, witnessed.'"* The expected answer is a valid Senion clause. Word order and the evidential are mandatory; comma placement and hyphen presence are tolerated.

The gate does **not** show you the questions in advance. You will not be told whether your answer is wrong until you submit it. A wrong answer terminates the sequence. You may begin again as many times as you wish.

## What the gate will not accept

- An answer that omits a required evidential.
- An answer in the original (pre-revision) **Subject-Verb-Object** word order. The working group's current canon is **Subject-Object-Verb** with a final verb cluster. If your reading reflects an older reconstruction, the gate will refuse it. (See *Master Reference* for the rationale.)
- An answer that translates a sentence by quoting the Roseta-stone or the bilingual memos verbatim. The verification material is held separately from the learning material and uses sentences you have not been given a translation for.

## What completion entitles you to

A staff member who passes all thirteen gates receives a single message addressed to them by the Office of Inherited Silences, sent over the channel the office maintains for verified Codicology contacts. The message identifies a subject line you are to use in your next correspondence with the office. Do not attempt to second-guess it.

The Office does not maintain a second channel, and there is no second message. If you do not receive the first, the gate did not pass.
