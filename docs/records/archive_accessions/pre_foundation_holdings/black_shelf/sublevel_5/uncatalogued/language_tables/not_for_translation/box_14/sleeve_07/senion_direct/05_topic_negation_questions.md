# 05 — Topic, Negation, and Questions

## Topic marker `-ha`

The clitic **`-ha`** attaches to a noun to mark it as the **topic** of the clause. English has no exact equivalent; the closest gloss is *as for X, …*.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor-ha, arvel-eth han-nai.** | As for the door, it is in the archive. I saw it. |
| **Arvel-ha, sel han-seln.** | As for the archive, silence exists. The record says so. |
| **Roseta-ha, glosul-eth unhan-vor-seln.** | As for Rosetta, it is not in the glossary room. [Sealed.] The record says so. |

The comma after `-ha` is an English-romanisation convenience. In the original script the topic boundary is marked by a small unfilled wedge that the working group has not yet standardised in romanisation.

Topic-marking is the **default construction in Foundation prose**. Almost every handbook sentence opens with a `-ha`-marked noun. You should expect it.

### Topic + postposition

A noun may be **postposition-marked and topic-marked at the same time**. `-ha` goes last.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Arvel-eth-ha, sel han-seln.** | As for "in the archive", silence exists. |
| **Kor-toh-ha, osher par-thel-thur.** | As for "toward the door", the witness will go. |

This is grammatical but uncommon; the simpler `Arvel-ha` reading is preferred.

## Negation: `un-`

Negation is a **prefix**, attached to the verb (most commonly) or the noun.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor unhan-thur.** | The door does not exist. I infer it. |
| **Osher kelhom unthuri-us-ren-nai.** | The witness did not read the record. I saw. |
| **Roseta unhan-vor-seln.** | Rosetta does not exist. [Sealed.] The record says so. |
| **Unmal arvel-eth han-seln.** | A not-room exists in the archive. The record says so. |
| **Sel-ha, unglosi-vor-seln.** | As for the silence, do not translate it. [Sealed.] The record says so. |

When `un-` attaches to a **noun**, it creates a *not-X*: a thing that, administratively or physically, *is not* that thing. The grammar is neutral; the Foundation's bureaucratic interpretation is documented in *Clause 19.0 Subsection: Administrative Absences*.

When `un-` attaches to a **verb**, it negates the clause. The evidential is still required.

### Negation pitfalls

- **`un-` is a prefix.** It comes *before* the root. *Kor han-un-seln* is not a Senion sentence.
- **Negation does not remove the evidential.** *Kor unhan* is not grammatical. *Kor unhan-thur* is.
- **Negation does not stack.** *Unun-* is meaningless — but the working group has spotted it in three Pre-Threshold registers and is undecided whether the scribes meant it ironically or were drunk.

## Imperatives

The Pre-Threshold Tongue does not have a dedicated imperative form. Commands are rendered with a **second-person subject** + the **sealed mood**, or by stripping the subject altogether when context is clear.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Na sel unglosi-vor-seln.** | (You) do not translate the silence. [Sealed.] |
| **Sel unglosi-vor-seln.** | (You) do not translate the silence. [Sealed.] (subject elided) |
| **Glosi-vor.** | Translate. [Sealed.] *(the marginal sentence on the Roseta-stone)* |

The marginal sentence **`Glosi-vor.`** is an imperative with **no evidential**, which the working group treats as ungrammatical at the surface level but possibly intentional. It is the only attested case in the corpus. **Do not imitate it.**

## Questions: `ma`

A question is formed by adding **`ma`** at the very end of the clause, after the evidential. The evidential is still required.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor han-seln ma?** | Does the door exist? (per the record) |
| **Osher kelhom thuri-us-ren-nai ma?** | Did the witness read the record? (you saw) |
| **Sūn nadi-us-ren-nal ma?** | Has the signal arrived? (you heard) |
| **Arvel-eth-ha, sel han-seln ma?** | As for "in the archive", does silence exist? |

A question without an evidential is ungrammatical; the working group treats it as a refusal to answer.

The question particle `ma` is **invariant**. It does not change for tense, mood, or evidential. A polite question and a rude question are both *ma*.
