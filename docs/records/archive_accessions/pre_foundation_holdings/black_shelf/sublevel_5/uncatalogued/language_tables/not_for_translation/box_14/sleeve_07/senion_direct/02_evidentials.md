# 02 — Evidentials

The most distinctive feature of the Pre-Threshold Tongue is its **required evidentiality**. Every declarative clause ends in a particle that states *how the speaker knows what they are saying*. To omit it is ungrammatical; the working group treats omission as evasive.

There are seven evidentials in the natural language.

## The seven

| Particle | Class | Used when the speaker… | English gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| **`-nai`** | direct visual | …saw it. | *(I saw)*, *(witnessed)* |
| **`-nal`** | non-visual sensory | …heard, smelled, felt, sensed it. | *(I heard)*, *(I felt)*, *(sensed)* |
| **`-thur`** | inferential | …deduced it from evidence. | *(apparently)*, *(I infer)*, *(by traces)* |
| **`-mar`** | assumptive | …assumes it from habit or general knowledge. | *(as usual)*, *(I expect)* |
| **`-kel`** | reportative | …was told by someone, or it was generally reported. | *(I'm told)*, *(reportedly)* |
| **`-sai`** | quotative | …is preserving the exact wording of another speaker. | *(quoted)*, *("…", they said)* |
| **`-seln`** | written / recorded | …read it in writing — register, inscription, handbook, log. | *(from the record)*, *(per the register)* |

## Worked contrast

The same proposition rendered seven ways:

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor morh-us-ren-nai.** | The door opened. I saw it. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-nal.** | The door opened. I heard / felt it. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-thur.** | The door opened, apparently. (I infer it.) |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-mar.** | The door opened, as expected. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-kel.** | The door opened, I'm told. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-sai.** | "The door opened," they said. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-seln.** | The door opened, according to the record. |

The choice of evidential does **not** modify the **truth** of the claim. Every one of these sentences may be true or false. The evidential states only the **source** of the claim.

This is the point where Stillwater staff most commonly mistranslate. The Foundation has, over time, come to treat `-seln` as more *authoritative* than the others. Native Senion makes no such ranking. The verification gate tests the native distinction, not the Foundation's overlay.

## When to use which

- Use **`-nai`** when you saw the event.
- Use **`-nal`** when you heard, smelled, or felt it. **Sound** in particular almost always takes `-nal`, not `-nai`.
- Use **`-thur`** when you see consequences but not the event itself. Marks on the floor, a door left ajar, the smell of fresh ink.
- Use **`-mar`** for habitual claims: routines, scheduled events, things that happen every Tuesday.
- Use **`-kel`** when the source is another person and the report is unsealed.
- Use **`-sai`** when you are quoting the other person's exact wording. Pair this with quotation marks in romanisation.
- Use **`-seln`** when the source is **written** — a register, an inscription, an internal handbook, an audit log. This is the most common evidential in Foundation prose.

## Notes on idiom

- A speaker may stack a tense or mood suffix between the root and the evidential, but the evidential remains last.
- An evidential **may not be repeated** within the same clause. *Kor han-nai-seln* is not a Senion sentence; it is what someone says when they are trying to be agreeable in two registers at once.
- An evidential **may not be replaced by `-vor`**. `-vor` is mood, not evidence. A clause must carry **both** if the speaker wants to mark sealing and source.

## Idiomatic conventions in Foundation prose

| Where you see it | What evidential to expect |
|---|---|
| Notices, posted on a board | `-seln` (the board is a record) |
| Memos, internal | `-seln` if formal; `-kel` if conversational |
| Field reports of witnessed events | `-nai` for visual events, `-nal` for audible |
| Inferences in incident write-ups | `-thur` |
| Routines, standing orders | `-mar` |
| Direct quotation in interview transcripts | `-sai` |

The verification gate weights `-nai` / `-kel` / `-thur` / `-seln` heavily; the other three (`-nal`, `-mar`, `-sai`) appear in only a few questions. You will not fail the gate for being weak on `-mar`, but you may be slowed by it.
