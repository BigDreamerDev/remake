### Notes left by the working group

- **Line 1**: *Bcy senen-ostr* is a date. *senen* you know (decimal 7). *-ostr* you may or may not have met — it is the postposition you saw on the stone (line IV, *nēr-aru*; *nēr* is *below*, *-aru* is *from*, *-ostr* is the next-of-kin) and it appears in the Foundation's Discordian dates regularly. You do not need to know *which* day it is to read the line.
- **Line 2**: All six positions are filled with the numerals you have. Line 2 is the easiest sentence in the document. The working group includes it for confidence.
- **Line 3**: *-aru* you know. *osh-* you know (the verb *to see / to witness*; you have met *osher*, the *one who witnesses*). The new word here is *oshul*; recognising the suffix is sufficient.
- **Line 4**: Resolves the witness's identity in Document α, line 3. *Read it back if you have not.*
- **Line 5**: Resolves the referent of *meren* in Document α, line 7. *glosul* is a derivation of *glosi-* using the suffix you know.
- **Line 6**: Read against line 2.
- **Line 7**: *par-* is a verb you may not have met. It is **on the slip-template** you were given in Memo α as one of the verbs to derive from the Old Norse and Basque columns of the stone. Look at stone line VI's β column (*Rosetta **er** í bókahúsi*) and stone line IV's γ column (*Seinalea behetik **dator***): *er* and *dator* both render Senion roots that you have already glossed. The verb *par-* is on neither of those lines; it is on a line whose β column begins *Fyrsta hurð **hefir opnazk***. Read it back. You will find the verb has two senses in Senion that are not the same in English.
- **Line 9**: A quotation. The marginal sentence on the Roseta-stone speaks for itself here.
- **Line 10**: Pattern Integrity is named as the recipient of the recorded source.
- **Line 11**: The closing line. It is shorter than the others.
