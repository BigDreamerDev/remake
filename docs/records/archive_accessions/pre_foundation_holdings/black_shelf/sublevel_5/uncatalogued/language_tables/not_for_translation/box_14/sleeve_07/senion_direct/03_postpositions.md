# 03 — Postpositions

Where English uses prepositions (*in*, *to*, *from*, *of*, etc.), Senion uses **postpositions** that attach to the noun with a hyphen.

## The closed set

| Form | English | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **`-eth`** | in, at, inside, within | `arvel-eth` = in the archive |
| **`-toh`** | to, toward | `kor-toh` = to the door |
| **`-aru`** | from, out of | `vehel-aru` = from the building |
| **`-en`** | of, belonging to | `tier-en` = of the rank |
| **`-bel`** | under, beneath, below | `besh-bel` = under the floor |
| **`-tor`** | beside, adjacent to | `mir-tor` = beside the mirror |
| **`-mar`** *(postposition)* | through, by way of | `vethr-mar` = through the passage |
| **`-dor`** | with, accompanied by | `kelh-dor` = with the record |
| **`-vesh`** | without, lacking | `sn-vesh` = without sound |
| **`-an`** | on, upon, against | `keth-an` = on the ceiling |
| **`-oth`** | before, in front of | `kor-oth` = before the door |
| **`-theloth`** | after, behind, following | `osher-theloth` = behind the witness |
| **`-ostr`** | next-of-kin, adjacent in series | `senen-ostr` = the day after Bcy 7 (used in dates) |

> **Watch the homophone.** `-mar` is *also* the **assumptive evidential**. In the verb cluster it sits at the end (`han-mar` = *exists, as expected*). On a noun it is the postposition (`vethr-mar` = *through the passage*). The two never collide in practice — one binds to verbs, the other to nouns — but the same letters are doing two jobs.

## Worked phrases

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **arvel-eth** | in the archive |
| **kor-toh** | to the door |
| **vehel-aru** | from the building |
| **arvel-en kor** | the archive's door |
| **besh-bel** | under the floor |
| **mir-tor** | beside the mirror |
| **kelh-dor** | with the record |
| **sn-vesh** | without sound |

## Stacking

A noun may carry **one** postposition. To express more complex relations, use **two noun phrases**, each with its own postposition, joined by the verb or by topic-marking.

| English | Senion |
|---|---|
| The door is in the archive, in the building. | **Kor arvel-eth vehel-eth han-nai.** |
| The witness comes from the archive into the room. | **Osher arvel-aru mal-toh nadi-or-nai.** |

## Postpositions and the topic marker

The topic clitic **`-ha`** attaches **after** any postposition.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Arvel-eth-ha, sel han-seln.** | As for in the archive, silence exists. (The archive is where silence lives.) |
| **Kor-toh-ha, osher par-thel-thur.** | As for toward the door, the witness will go. (The witness's path is toward the door.) |

This compound is uncommon outside official prose but is grammatical.

## Apparent prepositions to avoid

There is no Senion **of** that comes before the noun. There is no Senion **the**. There is no Senion **for** that takes an infinitive. If you find yourself wanting any of these, your English clause needs to be **shortened or restructured** before translation.
