# 1488 Customs Warrant — Dockside Witness Ledger

**Accession:** SW-ARC-VLM-1488-0151
**Custody:** Lower Reading Room
**Current Location:** Pondroom
**Material:** salt-stained card
**Hand:** late chancery
**Status:** unreadable after noon

## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a customs warrant and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1488, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: witness marks do not agree with index.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

A witness named in the margin appears in two separate centuries of the archive without change of address.

The catalogue number was assigned before the accession arrived, according to the receipt book.

A docket note refers to the blank visitor entry. This may be administrative noise.

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

## Handling

- If the room number appears in pencil, do not erase it.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.
