## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a court roll and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1869, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: pagination continues beyond final folio.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

The catalogue number was assigned before the accession arrived, according to the receipt book.

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

The lower margin contains a waterline that does not correspond to any known flood level.

A witness named in the margin appears in two separate centuries of the archive without change of address.

## Handling

- Ink that refuses ultraviolet inspection should be filed under patience.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.
