## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a sanatorium visitor book and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1996, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: seal impression wet to touch.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

A docket note refers to the pondroom return. This may be administrative noise.

The catalogue number was assigned before the accession arrived, according to the receipt book.

The item has been requested by a reading room that is not currently open to staff.

A historical seal appears in the correct position, but the impression is reversed, as if pressed from beneath the page.

## Handling

- Copies made after sunset must be returned face down.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.
