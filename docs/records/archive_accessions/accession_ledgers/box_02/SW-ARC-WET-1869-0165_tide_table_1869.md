# 1869 Tide Table — Lighthouse Keeper Memorandum

**Accession:** SW-ARC-WET-1869-0165
**Custody:** Wet Copy Intake
**Current Location:** Building Δ
**Material:** stitched pamphlet
**Hand:** brown ink hand
**Status:** original withheld

## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a tide table and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1869, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: date corrected by unknown hand.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

A witness named in the margin appears in two separate centuries of the archive without change of address.

The document references Saltmarsh as if it were a civic location rather than an internal Foundation designation.

Two folios use the same page number. Both have been treated as the original for different audits.

The lower margin contains a waterline that does not correspond to any known flood level.

## Handling

- Do not index a blank folio by the sound it makes.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.

## Extract

> The parish bell was heard beneath the paper. No bell is listed in the parish account.

## Archive Decision

Transfer a copy to Wet Copy Intake and leave the original where it believes it is.
