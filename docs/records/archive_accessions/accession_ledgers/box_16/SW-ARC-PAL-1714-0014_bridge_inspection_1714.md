## Catalogue Note

The item was received as a bridge inspection and is treated as a historical document for the purposes of Codicology review. The recorded date is 1714, though the document has been known to prefer adjacent years when stored in damp shelving.

Condition on intake: corners remember being folded.

The document should not be cleaned, flattened, warmed, sung near, or compared directly against any recollection supplied by the donor.

## Historical Summary

A witness named in the margin appears in two separate centuries of the archive without change of address.

The document references Driftmoor as if it were a civic location rather than an internal Foundation designation.

A historical seal appears in the correct position, but the impression is reversed, as if pressed from beneath the page.

The item has been requested by a reading room that is not currently open to staff.

## Handling

- Do not index a blank folio by the sound it makes.
- File copies separately from originals even when the original insists there is no copy.
- If the record becomes heavier after transcription, stop weighing it.
- Do not move this item to a brighter shelf to prove that the ink is ordinary.
