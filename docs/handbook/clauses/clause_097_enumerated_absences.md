# Clause 097 — Enumerated Absences

**Stillwater Foundation handbook fragment:** clause supplement
**Circulation:** Low-clearance / copied from damp master / do not laminate
**Responsible office:** Pattern Integrity Working Group
**Review state:** active until contradicted by a newer silence
**Local clause reference:** 9.7

This fragment describes ordinary conduct around the visitor log, the lower switchboard panel, and any other ordered series the Foundation enumerates and then declines to fill. It is intentionally over-specific because the Foundation has learned that vague instructions give rooms too much space to improvise. Staff should read the section once, then again from the reflection of the page if authorised, and then behave as if only the second reading was official.

The procedure is not a containment instruction and is not an emergency instruction. It is a way to keep the building from noticing the difference between an employee who understands and an employee who is only passing through. The difference matters to the building, though it must not be discussed in corridors with carpet older than the reader.

---

## 1. The Visitor Log

For any given day, the visitor log is to be ruled into a sequence of fourteen entries. The first six are to be signed by arriving visitors in the order of their arrival. The eighth through fourteenth are to be signed by arriving visitors in the order of their arrival after the seventh.

The seventh entry is to remain blank.

Do not sign the seventh entry.

Do not assign the seventh entry.

Do not look at the seventh entry once it has been left blank.

If the seventh entry has been signed, the day is to be filed as **closed**. No further entries are to be made. Visitors arriving after the seventh entry has been signed are to be directed, politely, to the corridor they came from.

If the seventh entry has been signed by a visitor who is no longer present, do not erase the signature. The day is still closed.

If the seventh entry has been signed by an employee, the employee is to be referred to the Office of Inherited Silences via Form D-17(b). The day remains closed.

---

## 2. The Lower Switchboard

The lower switchboard panel maintained by the Codicology Wing carries **fourteen rings** and **one lower receiver**. The receiver answers from one ring only. Staff who place calls through the panel are reminded that the rule for the visitor log applies, in inverted form, to the panel:

> The seventh entry is to remain blank in the log.
> The seventh ring is the only one the receiver answers from on the panel.

The two procedures are not unrelated. The Foundation does not currently advance a working theory as to why they share a number; the working group's position is that the building should not be asked.

Staff routing audio requests through the lower switchboard should consult *SW-HB-COD-019 — Lower Switchboard Audio Retrieval* for the full call shape.

## 3. Other Enumerated Absences

The following are also subject to this clause:

- The **seventh chair** at any conference table in building Δ is to remain unoccupied. If only seven chairs are available, one is to be added.
- The **seventh document** in any batch returned from Codicology archive intake is to remain unread until the other thirteen have been processed.
- The **seventh stair** on the east stairwell of building ྾2 is to be stepped over, not stepped on, between 17:00 and 19:00. Cymatic monitoring is in effect.
- The **seventh chime** of any clock observed to chime more than six times is to be disregarded. If the clock continues past seven, file Form V-9.

Other sevenths may apply. The working group does not maintain a comprehensive list; the practice is that an employee who notices a seventh is the employee who is meant to remember it.

---

## 4. Filing

Anomalies in any of the above series are to be filed with the Pattern Integrity Working Group via Form PI-7.

Form PI-7 itself has only six fields. There is no seventh field. Do not request one.
