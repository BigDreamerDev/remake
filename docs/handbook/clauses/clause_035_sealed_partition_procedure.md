# Clause 035 Sealed Partition Procedure

**Stillwater Foundation handbook fragment:** clause supplement
**Circulation:** Low-clearance / copied from damp master / do not laminate
**Responsible office:** Facilities Without Occupancy
**Review state:** active until contradicted by a newer silence
**Local clause reference:** 21.5.9

This fragment describes ordinary conduct around the unnumbered canteen queue. It is
intentionally over-specific because the Foundation has learned that vague instructions
give rooms too much space to improvise. Staff should read the section once, then again
from the reflection of the page if authorised, and then behave as if only the second
reading was official.

The procedure is not a containment instruction and is not an emergency instruction. It
is a way to keep the building from noticing the difference between an employee who
understands and an employee who is only passing through. The difference matters to the
building, though it must not be discussed in corridors with carpet older than the
reader.

Some records in this section appear to refer to commands, rooms, or forms that do not
appear elsewhere. This is expected. Handbook editions preserve obsolete interfaces so
that obsolete interfaces do not attempt to preserve themselves in staff behaviour.

If this text appears to be pointing at a single answer, close the file and check the
nearest clock. A useful-looking line is often a line trying to be followed. A followed
line is a corridor. Corridors are not permitted to supervise Trainee personnel.

## Staff conduct requirements

1. Do not remove wet floor signs from dry floors. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
2. Do not remove wet floor signs from dry floors. Cross-reference the nearest clause that does not face the window.
3. Do not press further lift buttons during the delay. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
4. Do not let a second person explain the first person's silence. Witnessed absence is not absence. It is attendance without geometry.
5. Do not dry paper that arrived damp around a name. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
6. Do not let a second person explain the first person's silence. If the entry appears useful, it is probably administrative residue and should be copied by hand.
7. Do not let a second person explain the first person's silence. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
8. Do not remove wet floor signs from dry floors. Retain for orientation only; do not escalate beyond Quiet-Water unless glass is uncovered.
9. Do not press further lift buttons during the delay. If the entry appears useful, it is probably administrative residue and should be copied by hand.
10. Do not count empty chairs twice. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
11. Do not answer from inside the pause. If the entry appears useful, it is probably administrative residue and should be copied by hand.
