# Clause 033 Blue Grey Threshold Procedure

**Stillwater Foundation handbook fragment:** clause supplement
**Circulation:** Low-clearance / copied from damp master / do not laminate
**Responsible office:** Mirror Discipline Unit
**Review state:** active until contradicted by a newer silence
**Local clause reference:** 29.9.7

This fragment describes ordinary conduct around the damp descaling sink. It is
intentionally over-specific because the Foundation has learned that vague instructions
give rooms too much space to improvise. Staff should read the section once, then again
from the reflection of the page if authorised, and then behave as if only the second
reading was official.

The procedure is not a containment instruction and is not an emergency instruction. It
is a way to keep the building from noticing the difference between an employee who
understands and an employee who is only passing through. The difference matters to the
building, though it must not be discussed in corridors with carpet older than the
reader.

Some records in this section appear to refer to commands, rooms, or forms that do not
appear elsewhere. This is expected. Handbook editions preserve obsolete interfaces so
that obsolete interfaces do not attempt to preserve themselves in staff behaviour.

If this text appears to be pointing at a single answer, close the file and check the
nearest clock. A useful-looking line is often a line trying to be followed. A followed
line is a corridor. Corridors are not permitted to supervise Trainee personnel.

## Staff conduct requirements

1. Do not press further lift buttons during the delay. Retain for orientation only; do not escalate beyond Quiet-Water unless glass is uncovered.
2. Do not let a second person explain the first person's silence. Retain for orientation only; do not escalate beyond Quiet-Water unless glass is uncovered.
3. Do not use your archived name in a reflected terminal. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
4. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. If the entry appears useful, it is probably administrative residue and should be copied by hand.
5. Do not thank the stairwell, even if it waits. Cross-reference the nearest clause that does not face the window.
6. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
7. Do not synchronise breathing with nearby terminals. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
8. Do not assume a room is safer because it is familiar. Destroy no copies. The duplicate may be the one that remembers the route.
9. Do not thank the stairwell, even if it waits. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
10. Do not synchronise breathing with nearby terminals. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
11. Do not thank the stairwell, even if it waits. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
12. Do not dry paper that arrived damp around a name. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
