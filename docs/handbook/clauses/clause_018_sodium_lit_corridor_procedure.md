# Clause 018 Sodium Lit Corridor Procedure

**Stillwater Foundation handbook fragment:** clause supplement
**Circulation:** Low-clearance / copied from damp master / do not laminate
**Responsible office:** Hydrology Survey Cell
**Review state:** active until contradicted by a newer silence
**Local clause reference:** 19.0.0

This fragment describes ordinary conduct around the quiet-water floor map. It is
intentionally over-specific because the Foundation has learned that vague instructions
give rooms too much space to improvise. Staff should read the section once, then again
from the reflection of the page if authorised, and then behave as if only the second
reading was official.

The procedure is not a containment instruction and is not an emergency instruction. It
is a way to keep the building from noticing the difference between an employee who
understands and an employee who is only passing through. The difference matters to the
building, though it must not be discussed in corridors with carpet older than the
reader.

Some records in this section appear to refer to commands, rooms, or forms that do not
appear elsewhere. This is expected. Handbook editions preserve obsolete interfaces so
that obsolete interfaces do not attempt to preserve themselves in staff behaviour.

If this text appears to be pointing at a single answer, close the file and check the
nearest clock. A useful-looking line is often a line trying to be followed. A followed
line is a corridor. Corridors are not permitted to supervise Trainee personnel.

## Staff conduct requirements

1. Do not thank the stairwell, even if it waits. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
2. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. Cross-reference the nearest clause that does not face the window.
3. Do not dry paper that arrived damp around a name. If the entry appears useful, it is probably administrative residue and should be copied by hand.
4. Do not assume a room is safer because it is familiar. Witnessed absence is not absence. It is attendance without geometry.
5. Do not count empty chairs twice. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
6. Do not read a chalkboard that begins reading you first. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
7. Do not dry paper that arrived damp around a name. Retain for orientation only; do not escalate beyond Quiet-Water unless glass is uncovered.
8. Do not use your archived name in a reflected terminal. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
9. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
10. Do not read a chalkboard that begins reading you first. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
11. Do not synchronise breathing with nearby terminals. Retain for orientation only; do not escalate beyond Quiet-Water unless glass is uncovered.
