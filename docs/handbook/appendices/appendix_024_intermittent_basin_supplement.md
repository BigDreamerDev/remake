# Appendix 024 Intermittent Basin Supplement

**Stillwater Foundation handbook fragment:** appendix
**Circulation:** Low-clearance / copied from damp master / do not laminate
**Responsible office:** Night Shift Liaison
**Review state:** active until contradicted by a newer silence
**Local clause reference:** 25.2.5

This fragment describes ordinary conduct around the stale handrail. It is intentionally
over-specific because the Foundation has learned that vague instructions give rooms too
much space to improvise. Staff should read the section once, then again from the
reflection of the page if authorised, and then behave as if only the second reading was
official.

The procedure is not a containment instruction and is not an emergency instruction. It
is a way to keep the building from noticing the difference between an employee who
understands and an employee who is only passing through. The difference matters to the
building, though it must not be discussed in corridors with carpet older than the
reader.

Some records in this section appear to refer to commands, rooms, or forms that do not
appear elsewhere. This is expected. Handbook editions preserve obsolete interfaces so
that obsolete interfaces do not attempt to preserve themselves in staff behaviour.

If this text appears to be pointing at a single answer, close the file and check the
nearest clock. A useful-looking line is often a line trying to be followed. A followed
line is a corridor. Corridors are not permitted to supervise Trainee personnel.

## Staff conduct requirements

1. Do not assume a room is safer because it is familiar. Retain for orientation only; do not escalate beyond Quiet-Water unless glass is uncovered.
2. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. If the entry appears useful, it is probably administrative residue and should be copied by hand.
3. Do not let a second person explain the first person's silence. Cross-reference the nearest clause that does not face the window.
4. Do not synchronise breathing with nearby terminals. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
5. Do not answer from inside the pause. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
6. Do not use your archived name in a reflected terminal. Witnessed absence is not absence. It is attendance without geometry.
7. Do not count empty chairs twice. When in doubt, continue walking until the next posted map contradicts itself.
8. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. Destroy no copies. The duplicate may be the one that remembers the route.
9. Do not file apologies under maintenance unless they are mechanical. Cross-reference the nearest clause that does not face the window.
10. Do not thank the stairwell, even if it waits. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
11. Do not assume a room is safer because it is familiar. Cross-reference the nearest clause that does not face the window.
