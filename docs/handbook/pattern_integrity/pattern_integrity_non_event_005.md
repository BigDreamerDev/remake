# Pattern Integrity Non Event 005

**Stillwater Foundation handbook fragment:** pattern integrity non-event
**Circulation:** Low-clearance / copied from damp master / do not laminate
**Responsible office:** Quiet-Water Branch
**Review state:** active until contradicted by a newer silence
**Local clause reference:** 22.0.8

This fragment describes ordinary conduct around the stale corridor. It is intentionally
over-specific because the Foundation has learned that vague instructions give rooms too
much space to improvise. Staff should read the section once, then again from the
reflection of the page if authorised, and then behave as if only the second reading was
official.

The procedure is not a containment instruction and is not an emergency instruction. It
is a way to keep the building from noticing the difference between an employee who
understands and an employee who is only passing through. The difference matters to the
building, though it must not be discussed in corridors with carpet older than the
reader.

Some records in this section appear to refer to commands, rooms, or forms that do not
appear elsewhere. This is expected. Handbook editions preserve obsolete interfaces so
that obsolete interfaces do not attempt to preserve themselves in staff behaviour.

If this text appears to be pointing at a single answer, close the file and check the
nearest clock. A useful-looking line is often a line trying to be followed. A followed
line is a corridor. Corridors are not permitted to supervise Trainee personnel.

## Staff conduct requirements

1. Do not dry paper that arrived damp around a name. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
2. Do not count empty chairs twice. Use Form S-00 for silence received, silence withheld, or silence returned warm.
3. Do not press further lift buttons during the delay. Destroy no copies. The duplicate may be the one that remembers the route.
4. Do not answer from inside the pause. Destroy no copies. The duplicate may be the one that remembers the route.
5. Do not let a second person explain the first person's silence. File as non-actionable unless the room repeats the observation in writing.
