# Drowned Corridor Orientation MW-032

revision: kept wet
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

31.3 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
92.4 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
34.2 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
64.9 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
65.4 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
85.8 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
36.8 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.

## Common False Positives

- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- weather heard through an open window during weather

## Escalation Triggers

- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- fog pools below table height
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
