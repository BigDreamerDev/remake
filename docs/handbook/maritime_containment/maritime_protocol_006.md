# Maritime Containment Standard MW-006

revision: grey
department: Maritime Deviation

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

56.6 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
28.8 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
46.7 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
99.7 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
84.7 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
73.4 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
18.4 No bell is to be answered from below deck.

## Common False Positives

- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- fog pools below table height
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
