# Fogbound Witness Handling MW-030

revision: B
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

38.5 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
97.8 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
68.6 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
93.6 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
44.9 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
39.5 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
12.7 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.

## Common False Positives

- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
