# Fogbound Witness Handling MW-034

revision: C
department: Salt Archive

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

74.1 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
40.6 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
70.6 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
91.8 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
17.8 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
68.9 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
83.9 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.

## Common False Positives

- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- weather heard through an open window during weather

## Escalation Triggers

- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- fog pools below table height
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
