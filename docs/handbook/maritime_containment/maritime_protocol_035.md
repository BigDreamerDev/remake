# Drowned Corridor Orientation MW-035

revision: B
department: Maritime Deviation

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

90.1 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
40.9 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
68.9 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
40.2 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
98.6 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
65.8 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
32.7 No bell is to be answered from below deck.

## Common False Positives

- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words

## Escalation Triggers

- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- fog pools below table height
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, compare against the cafeteria tide board. The classification
labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be decoded as
