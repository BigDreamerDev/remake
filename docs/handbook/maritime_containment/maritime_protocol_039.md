# Maritime Containment Standard MW-039

revision: grey
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

76.7 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
38.4 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
53.4 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
12.9 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
15.3 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
59.9 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
38.4 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Common False Positives

- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words

## Escalation Triggers

- fog pools below table height
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
