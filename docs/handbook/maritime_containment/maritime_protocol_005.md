# Fogbound Witness Handling MW-005

revision: kept wet
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

54.1 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
34.9 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
69.9 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
18.4 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
97.7 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
21.9 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
61.2 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.

## Common False Positives

- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard

## Escalation Triggers

- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- fog pools below table height
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, file under 'ordinary condensation' until it spells a name. The
classification labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be
decoded as operational instruction.
