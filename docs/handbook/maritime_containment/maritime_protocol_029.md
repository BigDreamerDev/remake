# Brine Evidence Packaging MW-029

revision: B
department: Night Safety

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

84.4 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
66.7 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
65.7 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
20.9 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
47.8 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
74.2 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
69.4 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.

## Common False Positives

- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- weather heard through an open window during weather

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- fog pools below table height
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
