# Drowned Corridor Orientation MW-023

revision: kept wet
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

40.3 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
87.9 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
80.4 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
46.5 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
21.7 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
67.1 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
15.9 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Common False Positives

- weather heard through an open window during weather
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- scheduled fire-suppression tests

## Escalation Triggers

- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- fog pools below table height
- salt appears above knee height without spray
