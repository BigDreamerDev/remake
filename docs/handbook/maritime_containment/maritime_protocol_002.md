# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-002

revision: grey
department: Salt Archive

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

21.4 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
93.4 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
31.9 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
66.3 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
19.6 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
52.6 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
69.2 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Common False Positives

- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
