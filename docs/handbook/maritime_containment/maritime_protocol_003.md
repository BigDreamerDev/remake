# Brine Evidence Packaging MW-003

revision: C
department: Night Safety

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

16.4 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
84.9 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
89.3 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
35.6 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
78.1 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
99.4 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
78.1 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.

## Common False Positives

- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- weather heard through an open window during weather

## Escalation Triggers

- salt appears above knee height without spray
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
