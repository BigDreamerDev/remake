# Drowned Corridor Orientation MW-056

revision: B
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

97.3 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
23.3 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
61.2 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
56.1 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
86.4 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
81.9 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
44.6 No bell is to be answered from below deck.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- scheduled fire-suppression tests

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- fog pools below table height
