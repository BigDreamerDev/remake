# Maritime Containment Standard MW-017

revision: B
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

71.9 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
84.2 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
44.1 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
51.5 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
55.3 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
76.2 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
12.7 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.

## Common False Positives

- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- scheduled fire-suppression tests

## Escalation Triggers

- salt appears above knee height without spray
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- fog pools below table height
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
