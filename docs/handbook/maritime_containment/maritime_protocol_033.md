# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-033

revision: grey
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

97.9 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
44.9 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
49.4 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
34.7 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
12.4 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
93.7 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
76.4 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- fog pools below table height
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, file under 'ordinary condensation' until it spells a name. The
classification labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be
decoded as operational instruction.
