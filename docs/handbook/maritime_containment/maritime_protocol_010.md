# Drowned Corridor Orientation MW-010

revision: C
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

41.5 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
75.9 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
96.5 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
93.1 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
82.9 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
66.5 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
16.7 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.

## Common False Positives

- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- fog pools below table height
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, mark as weather unless the rain falls upward. The classification
labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be decoded as
operational instruction.
