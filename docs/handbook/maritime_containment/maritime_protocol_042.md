# Maritime Containment Standard MW-042

revision: unratified
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

53.1 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
24.3 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
63.1 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
57.7 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
92.6 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
59.3 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
80.5 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Common False Positives

- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- scheduled fire-suppression tests

## Escalation Triggers

- fog pools below table height
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
