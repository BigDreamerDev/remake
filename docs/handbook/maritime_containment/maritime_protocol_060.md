# Salt-Mirror Safety MW-060

revision: C
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

30.2 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
15.4 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
21.3 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
92.5 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
63.6 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
24.6 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
77.8 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- fog pools below table height
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room

## Administrative Note
