# Lighthouse Response Card MW-027

revision: unratified
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

36.9 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
64.4 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
69.5 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
91.1 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
60.3 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
17.1 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
61.9 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person

## Escalation Triggers

- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- fog pools below table height
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- salt appears above knee height without spray

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, mark as weather unless the rain falls upward. The classification
labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be decoded as
operational instruction.
