# Maritime Containment Standard MW-058

revision: B
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

43.4 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
81.9 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
49.1 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
12.1 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
51.8 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
55.6 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
11.9 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- fog pools below table height
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
