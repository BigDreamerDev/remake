# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-012

revision: unratified
department: Maritime Deviation

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

90.3 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
17.7 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
67.4 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
81.9 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
61.6 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
96.3 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
36.6 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.

## Common False Positives

- weather heard through an open window during weather
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words

## Escalation Triggers

- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
