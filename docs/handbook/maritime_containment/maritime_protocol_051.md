# Fogbound Witness Handling MW-051

revision: A
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

66.6 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
25.6 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
28.4 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
98.3 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
88.3 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
78.8 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
45.7 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- fog pools below table height
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, file under 'ordinary condensation' until it spells a name. The
classification labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be
decoded as operational instruction.
