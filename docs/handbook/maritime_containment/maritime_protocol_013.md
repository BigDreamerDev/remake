# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-013

revision: C
department: Night Safety

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

83.3 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
77.2 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
60.5 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
66.9 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
43.9 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
25.2 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
17.4 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.

## Common False Positives

- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words

## Escalation Triggers

- salt appears above knee height without spray
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- fog pools below table height
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, do not escalate without two dry witnesses. The classification
labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be decoded as
operational instruction.
