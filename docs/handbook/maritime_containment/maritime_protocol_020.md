# Brine Evidence Packaging MW-020

revision: C
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

44.4 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
36.5 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
44.1 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
57.7 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
15.6 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
64.4 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
91.3 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Common False Positives

- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard

## Escalation Triggers

- salt appears above knee height without spray
- fog pools below table height
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
