# Brine Evidence Packaging MW-031

revision: A
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

47.2 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
95.2 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
25.7 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
22.4 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
22.8 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
74.9 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
81.6 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.

## Common False Positives

- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- salt appears above knee height without spray
