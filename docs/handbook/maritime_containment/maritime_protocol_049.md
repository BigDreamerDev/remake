# Salt-Mirror Safety MW-049

revision: kept wet
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

68.2 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
10.4 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
24.5 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
33.9 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
51.9 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
46.8 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
39.7 No bell is to be answered from below deck.

## Common False Positives

- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck

## Escalation Triggers

- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- fog pools below table height
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor

## Administrative Note

For filing purposes only, mark as weather unless the rain falls upward. The classification
labels in this document are intentionally bureaucratic and should not be decoded as
operational instruction.
