# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-047

revision: grey
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

35.5 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
60.8 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
20.1 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
97.2 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
28.3 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
15.1 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
25.3 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.

## Common False Positives

- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person

## Escalation Triggers

- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
