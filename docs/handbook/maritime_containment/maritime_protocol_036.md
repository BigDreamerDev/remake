# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-036

revision: A
department: Night Safety

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

48.7 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
70.3 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
32.4 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
93.1 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
43.7 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
76.7 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
69.6 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.

## Common False Positives

- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- weather heard through an open window during weather

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- fog pools below table height
- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
