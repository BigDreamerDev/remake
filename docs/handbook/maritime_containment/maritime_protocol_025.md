# Fogbound Witness Handling MW-025

revision: grey
department: Quiet-Water Registry

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

97.9 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
29.6 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
68.8 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
64.7 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
60.7 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
92.6 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.
54.9 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.

## Common False Positives

- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- scheduled fire-suppression tests
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person

## Escalation Triggers

- salt appears above knee height without spray
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
