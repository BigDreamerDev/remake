# Maritime Containment Standard MW-001

revision: unratified
department: Liminal Hydrology

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

13.7 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
31.8 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
77.3 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
95.6 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
46.4 Personnel hearing surf through a closed filing cabinet are to leave the room without speaking.
36.6 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
47.1 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.

## Common False Positives

- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- scheduled fire-suppression tests

## Escalation Triggers

- drains breathe in a recognisable rhythm
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- fog pools below table height
- salt appears above knee height without spray
