# Maritime Containment Standard MW-021

revision: C
department: Night Safety

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

39.9 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
89.6 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
93.3 If a hallway smells of kelp, count the doors but do not open the third.
89.4 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.
10.7 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
44.1 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
80.8 Mirrors facing standing water must be covered with cloth marked at all four corners.

## Common False Positives

- aquarium maintenance occurring in the actual aquarium
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain
- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- decorative nautical bells ringing after being struck

## Escalation Triggers

- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a lighthouse is visible from an internal corridor
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
- fog pools below table height
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- salt appears above knee height without spray
