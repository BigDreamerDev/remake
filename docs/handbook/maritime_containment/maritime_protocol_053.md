# Indoor Tide Protocol MW-053

revision: B
department: Maritime Deviation

## Purpose

This guidance supports staff assigned to sea-adjacent, water-adjacent, or water-pretending
events. It is written for rooms that flood without plumbing, shores that arrive inside
buildings, and records that smell of salt after filing.
## General Conduct

94.9 If a window shows open sea where a wall is expected, draw the curtain and file Form MW-13.
88.3 Do not enter water that reflects a ceiling not present above you.
33.4 Do not record your own name in a wet logbook.
41.1 Buckets must not be used for collection unless the bucket was dry before sunrise.
19.7 No bell is to be answered from below deck.
23.7 Shoes found filled with seawater are not to be worn, dried, or returned.
58.1 If the tide arrives indoors, step backward until carpet is visible.

## Common False Positives

- ordinary pipe condensation spelling no complete words
- cafeteria mops stored in the correct cupboard
- weather heard through an open window during weather
- spillages with a known cup, bottle, or person
- staff shoes wet from documented outdoor rain

## Escalation Triggers

- a witness remembers being on a boat they never boarded
- fog pools below table height
- footprints arrive from the dry side of a locked door
- a door sounds like a shoreline when knocked
- salt appears above knee height without spray
- water reflects a space not accessible from the present room
