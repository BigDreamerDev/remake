## 1. What this packet is

This packet is the **Discovery-track** introduction to the Pre-Threshold Tongue (Senionsoln). It is one of two parallel tracks the working group is trialling. If you have been issued a slip directing you to the **Direct track**, this packet is not for you; return it to the desk and request the structured pack instead.

The Discovery track does not give you the language. It gives you the stone.

You will be expected to derive the language from the stone, from the bilingual memos that follow, and finally from monolingual archive material that uses what you have learned. The working group's position is that staff who reconstruct the grammar themselves are markedly less likely to mistranslate sealed material in the field.

## 2. What was received

The artefact was received without donor, country of origin, or declared language. Codicology's first assessment classified it as **not a language table**. The Archive's countersignature classified that classification as **not our decision**. Hydrology's standing assessment is **dry**.

What we have:

- One stone (small; can be lifted by one person with assistance).
- Five inscribed columns. Four are bodies of running text. The fifth is a script we have not yet placed and have catalogued under its register entry only: **Mycenaean Linear B**. The transliteration to Latin characters is preserved alongside.
- A scribal preface, partially defaced, identifying the columns as variants of a single utterance set.

The four body columns are catalogued as follows.

| Column | Identification | Codicology note |
|---|---|---|
| α | The Pre-Threshold Tongue (**Senionsoln**). | The language we are here to learn. Recorded in romanisation. |
| β | **Old Norse**, archaic. | A historical natural language. You may consult external resources. |
| γ | **Basque (Euskara)**, archaic. | A historical natural language. You may consult external resources. |
| δ | **Mycenaean Greek** in the syllabic script known as **Linear B**, with a Latin transliteration beneath. | A historical natural language. You may consult external resources. |

There is no English column on the stone.

## 3. Reading-room rules

1. **Do not translate the stone in the room that supplied it.** This is the standing instruction for the Black Shelf and is repeated here for completeness.
2. **Submit emendations via Form C-7** to the Codicology reading room. The working group will rule at the next sitting.
3. **You may use external dictionaries** for Old Norse, Basque, and Mycenaean Greek. The Foundation does not maintain reference copies of these languages on this floor.
4. **You may not refer to the `english_private` column.** No such column exists in the issued packet. If a copy of the stone is brought to you that bears a fifth column in English, return it unread to the desk; this is a marked dossier and will be retrieved by Pattern Integrity.
5. **Form a vocabulary of your own.** A blank slip is appended to your packet (Form SW-VOC-01). You are expected to fill it in as you proceed.

## 4. Sequence of materials

Read the packet in order. The sequence is graded.

| # | Document | What you should leave it knowing |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | Rosetta Transcript (this stone, four columns) | Roughly twenty content words; the shape of the verb cluster; two of the seven evidentials in context. |
| 02 | Paired Memo α (English/Senion bilingual) | The four core evidentials. How postpositions attach. |
| 03 | Paired Memo β (English/Senion bilingual) | Past tense. Sealed mood. Numerals. |
| 04 | Paired Memo γ (English/Senion bilingual) | Topic marker. Negation. The full evidential set in passing. |
| 05 | Archive Document α (Senion only) | Twenty new words you have never been told. Most are inferable. |
| 06 | Archive Document β (Senion only) | Synthesis. You will need everything above. |
| 07 | Verification Notice | How to present yourself at `senion.verify`. |

## 5. What completion looks like

You will know you are ready when you can read a sentence in the Pre-Threshold Tongue and tell another member of staff **where the speaker's knowledge came from**, not only what they said. Senion treats source as part of the sentence. The Foundation treats source as part of the clearance.

The two are not the same thing. Holding them apart is the discipline this packet is meant to instil.
