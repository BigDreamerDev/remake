## A. The topic marker `-ha`

The clitic **`-ha`** marks a noun as the **topic** of the clause — the thing the sentence is *about*. English does not have a direct equivalent; the closest gloss is *as for X, …*. In Foundation handbook prose the topic is overwhelmingly the most common construction; you will see it everywhere once you start reading the registers.

| English | Senion |
|---|---|
| 1. As for the door, it is in the archive. I saw it. | **Kor-ha, arvel-eth han-nai.** |
| 2. As for the archive, the name is there. The record says so. | **Arvel-ha, meren han-seln.** |
| 3. As for the witness, they read the record. The record says so. | **Osher-ha, kelhom thuri-or-seln.** |

**Working-group note.** The comma after `-ha` is an English-romanisation convenience. The original script marks the topic boundary by a small unfilled wedge that the working group has not yet found a romanisation for. The comma is good enough for our purposes.

A topic-marked clause is *not* a question. (Questions take a different particle — see section D.)

## B. Negation: `un-`

Negation is a **prefix** on the word it negates. Most often it attaches to the verb, but it can attach to a noun to derive a *not-X*.

| English | Senion |
|---|---|
| 4. The door does **not** exist. I infer it. | **Kor unhan-thur.** |
| 5. As for the silence, do **not** translate it. The record says so. | **Sel-ha, unglosi-vor-seln.** |
| 6. A **not-room** exists in the archive. The record says so. | **Unmal◇ arvel-eth han-seln.** |
| 7. The witness did **not** read the record. I saw it. | **Osher kelhom unthuri-us-ren-nai.** |

**Working-group note.** Line 5 is a near-quotation of stone line V. You have now read the stone.

`unmal` is a *not-room*: a room that is not, administratively or physically, a room. Foundation usage is broader than the literal sense — see the catalogue of `unmal`-classifications in *Clause 19.0 Subsection: Administrative Absences*. The grammar does not pin down the metaphysics; the handbook does.

## C. The full natural evidential set

Senion has **seven** evidentials. You have used four. The remaining three are introduced here.

| Particle | Class | Used when the speaker… |
|---|---|---|
| `-nai` | visual | …saw the thing directly. |
| `-nal`◇ | non-visual sensory | …heard, smelled, felt it. |
| `-thur` | inferential | …deduced it from evidence. |
| `-mar`◇ | assumptive | …assumes from habit or general knowledge. |
| `-kel` | reportative | …was told by another. |
| `-sai`◇ | quotative | …is preserving someone's exact wording. |
| `-seln` | written/recorded | …read it in a record. |

**Working-group note.** The natural seven are how a Senion speaker uses the language. The Foundation tends to weight `-seln` above the rest, treating *what is in the record* as more reliable than what was witnessed. The original language does no such thing. The evidential marks the **source** of a claim; it does not rank the **truth** of one. Both can be true. Both can be false.

That distinction is exactly what the verification gate is testing for.

| English | Senion |
|---|---|
| 8. There is a sound. I hear it. | **Sūn han-nal.** |
| 9. The signal arrived, as usual. | **Sūn nadi-us-ren-mar.** |
| 10. "The door opened," she said. | **«Kor morh-us-ren», kelh-us-ren-sai.** |

## D. Questions

A question is formed by **`ma`** placed at the end of the clause, after the evidential. The evidential remains obligatory.

| English | Senion |
|---|---|
| 11. Does the door exist (according to the record)? | **Kor han-seln ma?** |
| 12. Did the witness read the record (you saw)? | **Osher kelhom thuri-us-ren-nai ma?** |
| 13. Has the signal arrived (you heard)? | **Sūn nadi-us-ren-nal ma?** |

A question without an evidential is ungrammatical and treated as a refusal to answer. Do not write one.

## F. What changes from here

From the next document onward, the English column disappears. You will be reading the registers as the scribes wrote them.

What you should take from these three memos:

- **Vocabulary**: roughly forty words. You will encounter about twenty more in the monolingual documents; most are derivable.
- **Verb cluster**: ROOT — (aspect) — (tense) — (mood) — evidential. The evidential is the only mandatory piece.
- **Postpositions**: `-eth` / `-aru` / `-toh`. More appear later but the principle is the same.
- **Topic**: `-ha` precedes the rest of the clause.
- **Negation**: `un-` prefixes the verb or the noun.
- **Questions**: `ma` ends the clause.

If any of these is unclear, repeat Memos α, β, γ in order before continuing. The working group **does not penalise re-reading**. The working group penalises field translations submitted by staff who did not re-read and should have.
