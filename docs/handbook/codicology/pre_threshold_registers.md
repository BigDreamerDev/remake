# The Senion-Square Tradition

## Notes on the codicological conventions of the Pre-Threshold registers

The Stillwater archive holds, in its lower stacks, a number of
manuscript registers conventionally grouped under the designation
**Pre-Threshold**. Their provenance is disputed; their dating is
unstable; and the identities of the scribes are not recorded.
Despite this, they share a number of codicological features so
consistent across the tradition that they merit dedicated treatment
in any working handbook of the Foundation's archival material.

### The leaf

Each leaf of a Pre-Threshold register bears, in its outer margin, a
chart of **thirty-six cells** in a square configuration. Within
these cells the scribe has set down, in a hand markedly steadier
than that of the body text, a single glyph per cell. No cell is left
vacant; no cell holds more than one glyph; and the disposition of
glyphs across the chart is uniform from leaf to leaf and from
register to register.

The Foundation does not, at present, hold a definitive account of
why the scribes chose to fix this convention so rigidly. Earlier
conjectures — that the chart is a *concordance*, a *foliation key*,
or a *mnemonic device for the recitation of the body text* — have
each in their turn been advanced and in their turn been found
insufficient.

### The marginal pairings

In the gutter margin, opposite each significant word of the body
text, the scribes have placed a sequence of **paired figures**, the
first and second of each pair lying always between one and six. The
pairings are unspaced and unpunctuated. Read in isolation they
appear to be a register of folio references. Read against the chart
in the outer margin, they may be something more.

What can be said for certain is that the pairings are not random.
Their distribution within each leaf is too even, and their range too
tightly bounded, to be the product of unstructured marginalia.

A further regularity bears noting. The pairings are organised
throughout into **runs of five** — five pairs at a time, set down in
a single uninterrupted band before the next band begins. The bands
are not interleaved within the marginalia of a single body word, nor
do they straddle the boundary between one significant word and the
next. The five is invariant across the tradition. No surviving
prefatory note remarks upon it.

### Provenance of the designation

The term *Pre-Threshold* is recent and is not used by the scribes
themselves, whose own designation for the tradition (if they had
one) is not preserved. The convention is the Foundation's, adopted
to distinguish these registers from the post-audit material catalogued
elsewhere in the lower stacks. The boundary between the two is
defined by the audit; the audit is defined by reference to a date
that has not yet occurred.

### Restricted access

Pre-Threshold registers are catalogued under Rank Codicology. Direct
consultation requires a reading-room key issued by the Office of
Inherited Silences and is permitted for a single sitting only. Notes
taken during consultation are not to be removed from the reading
room. Photographs are not permitted at any rank.

The chart in the outer margin may be transcribed by hand into the
reader's own notebook. The pairings may not.
