# 06 — Pronouns and Numerals

## Pronouns

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **ya** | I |
| **na** | you (singular) |
| **yalan** | we (inclusive) — includes the addressee |
| **nalan** | we (exclusive) — does not include the addressee |
| **ka** | you (plural) |
| **ten** | they |
| **ha** *(also: demonstrative)* | this / here |
| **to** *(also: demonstrative)* | that / there |

### Inclusive vs. exclusive *we*

The **inclusive / exclusive** distinction in the first-person plural is obligatory. A Senion speaker may **not** simply say *we* and let the listener guess.

- **yalan** — *we, including you*. Use when the addressee is part of the group being referred to.
- **nalan** — *we, not including you*. Use when the addressee is *not* in the group.

Foundation documents that translate English *we* without consideration often fall back on **nalan** by default. The working group considers this rude and is gradually correcting the practice.

### Pronoun + postposition

Pronouns take postpositions like any other noun.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **ya-toh** | to me |
| **na-aru** | from you |
| **yalan-eth** | among us (inclusive) |
| **ten-en** | of them |

## Numerals — base six (senary)

Senion counts in **base six**, consistent with the 6×6 organisation of the registers' charts. The numerals follow a regular compounding pattern after the first six.

| Decimal | Senary | Senion |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | **en** |
| 2 | 2 | **dū** |
| 3 | 3 | **thren** |
| 4 | 4 | **korv** |
| 5 | 5 | **pem** |
| 6 | 10 | **sen** |
| 7 | 11 | **senen** |
| 8 | 12 | **sendū** |
| 9 | 13 | **senthren** |
| 10 | 14 | **senkorv** |
| 11 | 15 | **senpem** |
| 12 | 20 | **dūsen** |
| 13 | 21 | **dūsenen** |
| 14 | 22 | **dūsendū** |
| 15 | 23 | **dūsenthren** |
| 18 | 30 | **thrensen** |
| 24 | 40 | **korvsen** |
| 30 | 50 | **pemsen** |
| 36 | 100 | **senion-ostr** |

The word **senion** itself — *the Tongue of the Six* — encodes the base. Numbers exceeding **senpem-senpem** (decimal 35) are rare in the corpus.

### Classifiers

Numerals require a **classifier** drawn from a closed set of six. The classifier specifies what kind of thing is being counted.

| Classifier | Counts |
|---|---|
| **`-mor`** | people / witnesses / staff |
| **`-fol`** | pages, folios, sheets |
| **`-mal`** | rooms, chambers, defined spaces |
| **`-kelh`** | records, accounts, written items |
| **`-sn`** | sounds, signals, instances of audible events |
| **`-ar`** | objects (default; used when nothing more specific applies) |

The classifier attaches to the **numeral**, not to the noun.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **osher sen-mor** | six witnesses |
| **kelhom thren-kelh** | three records |
| **mal dū-mal** | two rooms |
| **fol senen-fol** | seven pages |
| **sūn pem-sn** | five signals |
| **kor korv-ar** | four doors |

### Ordinals

There is no separate ordinal series. The pattern is **NOUN + numeral + `-eth`** ("the X-eth NOUN"): *the first door* is **kor en-eth**, *the second* is **kor dū-eth**, and so on.

## Pronouns in clauses — worked examples

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Ya kor osh-us-ren-nai.** | I saw the door. |
| **Na kelhom thuri-thel-mar.** | You will read the record (as usual). |
| **Yalan arvel-eth han-seln.** | We (incl. you) are in the archive. The record says so. |
| **Nalan kor-toh par-or-nai.** | We (excl. you) are going to the door. I see it. |
| **Ten osher-en kelhom thuri-or-kel.** | They are reading the witness's record, I'm told. |
