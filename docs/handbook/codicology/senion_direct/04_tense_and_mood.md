# 04 — Tense and Sealed Mood

The verb cluster has dedicated slots for **aspect**, **tense**, and **mood**. They sit between the verb root and the (mandatory) evidential.

```
ROOT  ( -ASPECT )  ( -TENSE )  ( -MOOD )  -EVIDENTIAL
```

## Aspect: `-or` and `-us`

Aspect tells you whether the action is **ongoing** or **completed**.

| Marker | Meaning | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| **`-or`** | imperfective | ongoing, habitual, *is doing* |
| **`-us`** | perfective | completed, *has done*, *did* |
| (none) | unmarked | a state, or a single instance with no aspect view |

Examples:

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor morh.** *(rare on its own)* | The door opens. |
| **Kor morh-or-nai.** | The door is opening / opens. I see it. |
| **Kor morh-us-nai.** | The door has opened. I saw it. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-nai.** | The door opened. I saw it. (past + completed) |
| **Osher kelhom thuri-or-seln.** | The witness is reading the record. The record says so. |
| **Osher kelhom thuri-us-ren-kel.** | The witness has read the record (past, completed). I'm told. |

You will almost always see at least one of the aspect markers on a verb that has tense. A bare root + tense + evidential (`morh-ren-nai`) is grammatical but rare and feels clipped.

## Tense: `-ren` and `-thel`

| Marker | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **`-ren`** | past |
| **`-thel`** | future |
| (none) | present / habitual |

Examples:

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor morh-or-nai.** | The door opens (now). |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-nai.** | The door opened. |
| **Kor morh-thel-seln.** | The door will open. The record says so. |
| **Osher kelhom thuri-thel-mar.** | The witness will read the record, as expected. |

Senion does not distinguish *yesterday* from *long ago*: all past is `-ren`. Likewise *tomorrow* and *eventually* are both `-thel`. Specific dates are given by postpositional phrases (often with `-ostr`).

## Sealed mood: `-vor`

The mood marker **`-vor`** attaches **after tense** and **before the evidential**. It marks the clause as **sealed** — restricted from repetition outside the speaker's clearance ring.

| Senion | English |
|---|---|
| **Kor han-seln.** | The door exists. The record says so. |
| **Kor han-vor-seln.** | The door exists. [Sealed.] The record says so. |
| **Kor morh-us-ren-vor-nai.** | The door opened. [Sealed.] I saw it. |
| **Sūn nadi-thel-vor-seln.** | The signal will come. [Sealed.] The record says so. |

`-vor` does **not** make a claim secret in the modern sense. It marks the claim as belonging to a class whose repetition outside clearance is procedurally restricted. The claim itself may be true or false; the seal is procedural.

In ARG / Foundation prose, you will see `-vor` on:

- Restricted invocations (Clause 11.3).
- Sealed audio records.
- Memorial observances during the silence.
- Witness statements taken under clearance.

You will **not** see `-vor` on:

- Routine memos.
- Public notices.
- Anything posted in a corridor.

## Pitfalls

- **Do not use `-vor` as the final word of a clause.** It must be followed by an evidential. *Kor han-vor* alone is not a sentence; it is what someone says when they have stopped speaking mid-clause.
- **`-vor` is not negation.** Negation is the prefix `un-` (see `05_topic_negation_questions.md`).
- **Aspect is not tense.** `-us` (completed) is not the same as `-ren` (past). A sentence may be completed in the present (`morh-us-nai`, *has just opened, I see it*) or completed in the past (`morh-us-ren-nai`, *had opened, I saw it*).
