# 09 — Verification Notice

You are now eligible to attempt verification at the Codicology gate.

## How to present

At a comShell terminal, invoke:

```
senion.verify
```

The gate will introduce itself, then put **thirteen** questions to you in sequence.

## What the gate asks

The thirteen questions are drawn from five families. The mix is consistent between sittings.

1. **Particle identification.** *"What does `-nai` indicate?"* Answer with a short phrase or a single word — *witnessed*, *seen*, *direct evidence*, etc.
2. **Postposition identification.** *"In `arvel-eth`, the suffix `-eth` indicates…"* Answer with a short gloss — *in*, *inside*, *at*.
3. **Tense or mood identification.** *"The suffix `-vor` adds what to the clause?"* Answer with one of the standard glosses — *sealed*, *restricted*, etc.
4. **Translation, Senion → English.** *"Translate: `Kor han-seln.`"* Answer with the English meaning in any clear phrasing. The gate accepts a wide range of wordings of the same sense — *the door exists, from the record*, *the door is open according to the register*, etc.
5. **Composition, English → Senion.** *"Render in Senion: 'The door exists, witnessed.'"* Answer with a valid Senion clause. Word order and the evidential are mandatory; comma placement and hyphen presence are tolerated.

## What the gate will not accept

- An answer that **omits a required evidential**.
- An answer in the **deprecated SVO word order**. The gate is set to the revised SOV canon. If your reading reflects the older reconstruction, the gate will refuse it.
- A translation that quotes the **handbook examples verbatim**. The gate uses sentences that do not appear in either track.
- An answer in a language other than English (for translations) or Senion (for compositions). The gate will not understand a Basque hint.

## What completion entitles you to

A staff member who passes all thirteen gates receives one message addressed to them by the Office of Inherited Silences. The message identifies a subject line for the staff member's next correspondence with the Office. The Office does not maintain a second channel.

## If you fail

A refused answer terminates the sequence. You may begin again as many times as you wish; the gate does not log refusals against you. The working group, however, **does** track which questions tend to refuse staff most often, and uses the data to revise the teaching pack. If you find yourself failing repeatedly on a particular question, your refusals are useful to the working group and ought to be filed via Form C-7.

## If you have not used either track

You may attempt the gate without preparation. The working group does not require staff to have used either pack. Staff who attempt the gate without preparation are statistically very unlikely to pass, but the gate is open.
